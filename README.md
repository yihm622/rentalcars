## How Pricing Actually Works

Here's the thing about car rental pricing - it's about as predictable as weather forecasts. Prices fluctuate based on location, season, day of the week, how far in advance you book, and probably the alignment of the planets.

But here's what we know:

**Best time to book**: Within the same week of your trip, you can sometimes find deals around $39/day on average. However, booking too last-minute during peak seasons can backfire spectacularly.

**Cheapest periods**: Based on historical data, you'll find the deepest discounts during:
- Summer Sale in June (up to 70-75% off)
- New Year's Sale in late December
- Random promotional periods throughout the year

**Economy cars** start around $25-40 per day in most major US cities, while **SUVs and luxury vehicles** can range from $50-150+ per day depending on location and availability.

## Insurance: The Necessary Evil

Here's where things get complicated. When you rent through Rentalcars.com, the base price typically includes:
- Collision Damage Waiver (CDW)
- Theft Protection
- Basic liability coverage

But here's what they don't tell you upfront: there's often a deductible (excess). If you damage the car, you might still be on the hook for $500-$2,000 depending on the rental company.

**Your options:**
1. **Use your credit card coverage** - Many credit cards offer rental car insurance if you decline the rental company's coverage and pay with that card
2. **Buy full coverage through Rentalcars.com** - Extra peace of mind, usually $10-30/day
3. **Decline everything and hope for the best** - Not recommended unless you enjoy financial Russian roulette

Before you arrive at the counter, check what your credit card actually covers. Call them, don't just assume.

## What to Know Before You Pick Up

**Required Documents:**
- Driver's license (held for at least 1-2 years)
- Credit card in the main driver's name (debit cards often not accepted)
- Booking voucher
- Passport (for international rentals)

**Age Requirements:**
- Minimum age: Usually 21, but can be 25 for luxury vehicles
- Under 25? Expect a young driver surcharge ($15-35/day)
- Over 70? Some companies charge senior fees or won't rent to you at all

**At the Counter:**
They'll try to upsell you on:
- "Upgrade" to a bigger car
- Additional insurance packages
- Prepaid fuel (usually overpriced)
- Toll passes

Feel free to decline if you don't need them. You've already paid, and you're not obligated to buy extras.

## Customer Reviews: The Reality Check

Let's talk about the elephant in the room. Customer reviews for Rentalcars.com are all over the place.

**Trustpilot**: Mixed reviews, with common complaints about:
- Booking not honored at pickup
- Disputes over refunds
- Communication issues between Rentalcars and actual rental companies
- Hidden charges appearing at the counter

**Positive feedback** usually mentions:
- Good prices
- Easy booking process
- Wide selection of vehicles
- Helpful customer service (when you can reach them)

Here's my take: Rentalcars.com is a comparison platform, not the actual rental company. When things go smoothly, it's great. When problems arise, you're dealing with two entities (Rentalcars and the rental company), and that can get messy.

**Best practices to avoid problems:**
1. Read ALL the terms before booking
2. Take photos/video of the car before driving off
3. Arrive during business hours at staffed locations
4. Keep all documentation
5. If using a promo code, screenshot the confirmation showing the discount applied
